How it works

ChanceMe takes a holistic, but quantitative, review of your college application and tries to calculate the likelihood you'll be accepted to certain schools.
The machine learning algorithm is trained on the portfolios of past students who have publicly shared their stats.
Although this project is still in the beginning stages, we are working on getting more data on different colleges and student portfolios so that the predictions get more accurate in the future!
To get your own results, fill out the form that's linked in the About section.

Factors analyzed by the algorithm:


  • Gender

  • Ethnicity

  • Test Scores

  • Intended Major(s)

  • Extracurriculars

  • Awards

About

ChanceMe is made by students, for students.
A lot of people are interested in what schools they might be qualified to get accepted into, and this project is looking to see how acurate a machine learning model could get in terms of predicting this. However, it won't be perfect since it doesn't take essays and recommendations into account. It will try to do the best it can given a student's quantitative specs, like test scores and demographics.
This project is inspired by the subreddit /r/chanceme where people give feedback to others based on their stats.
